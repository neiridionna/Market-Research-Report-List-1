<p><p>Composite materials in the automotive market primarily include Sheet Molding Compound (SMC), Fiber-Reinforced Polymer (FRP), and Resin Transfer Molding (RTM). SMC is used for producing strong, lightweight parts through a compression molding process, ideal for complex shapes. FRP utilizes fibers in a polymer matrix, enhancing strength while reducing weight, suitable for various components. RTM involves injecting resin into a closed mold containing dry fibers, allowing for intricate designs and optimal strength-to-weight ratios, making these composites advantageous for modern automotive applications.</p></p>
