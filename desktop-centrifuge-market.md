<p><p>The desktop centrifuge market is categorized into low-speed and high-speed segments based on operational capabilities. Low-speed centrifuges typically operate at speeds up to 5,000 RPM and are ideal for separating blood components or other biological fluids. In contrast, high-speed centrifuges reach speeds exceeding 10,000 RPM, allowing for the efficient separation of smaller particles and more complex samples. Each type serves distinct laboratory needs, influencing their demand and applications within clinical, research, and educational settings.</p></p>
