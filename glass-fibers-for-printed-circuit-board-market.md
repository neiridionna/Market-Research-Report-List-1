<p><p>Glass fibers used in printed circuit boards (PCBs) primarily include E-Glass, S-Glass, and other specialized types. E-Glass fiber is the most common, offering good electrical insulation and mechanical strength at a reasonable cost, making it suitable for standard PCBs. S-Glass fiber, on the other hand, provides superior strength and thermal resistance, ideal for high-performance applications. Other glass fiber types may include C-Glass or D-Glass, catering to specific engineering requirements, influencing thermal properties, weight, and overall circuit board performance.</p></p>
