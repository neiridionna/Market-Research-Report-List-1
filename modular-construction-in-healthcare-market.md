<p><p>Modular construction in the healthcare market involves prefabricated building methods that enhance efficiency and flexibility. Permanent modular construction refers to structures designed for long-term use, built off-site and then transported for assembly, ideal for hospitals and clinics. Relocatable modular construction, on the other hand, involves temporary facilities that can be moved as needed, supporting interim healthcare needs or expansions. Both approaches offer faster construction times, cost savings, and the ability to adapt to changing healthcare demands.</p></p>
