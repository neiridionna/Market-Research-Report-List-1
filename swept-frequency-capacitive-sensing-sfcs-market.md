<p><p>Swept Frequency Capacitive Sensing (SFCS) is a technique used to detect changes in capacitance caused by various factors, such as object proximity or material type. The market comprises different types: Surface Capacitance focuses on detecting touch on a surface layer, Projected Capacitance enables multi-touch capabilities over larger areas, Self-Capacitance measures individual sensor capacitance changes, while Mutual Capacitance assesses the interaction between paired sensors. Each type caters to distinct applications in consumer electronics, automotive, and industrial sectors, enhancing user interaction and sensing accuracy.</p></p>
