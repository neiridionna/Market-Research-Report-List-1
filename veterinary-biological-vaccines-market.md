<p><p>The veterinary biological vaccines market comprises three main types: live attenuated vaccines, inactivated vaccines, and others. Live attenuated vaccines contain weakened forms of pathogens, stimulating robust immune responses without causing disease. Inactivated vaccines consist of killed pathogens, offering safety and stability but often requiring multiple doses. The "others" category includes recombinant vaccines and subunit vaccines, which utilize specific antigens to provoke an immune response. Together, these vaccine types play a crucial role in preventing infectious diseases in animals, safeguarding public health and the agricultural industry.</p></p>
